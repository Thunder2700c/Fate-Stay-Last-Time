We drove north, leaving the flat, suffocating heat of Punjab behind. The highway stretched out like a dark ribbon under the moon. As the hours bled away, the terrain began to shift. The sprawling urban sprawl gave way to the foothills, and then, the towering, jagged silhouettes of the Himalayas.

The temperature dropped rapidly. I turned the SUV's heater on, watching my breath fog the glass.

By Hour 45, the paved roads ended. We were driving on treacherous, snow-covered dirt paths hugging the edge of sheer drops. The air grew thin. The sky began to lighten, casting a pale, icy blue glow over the snow-capped peaks.

Archer manifested in the back seat. He leaned forward, his gray eyes scanning the desolate, frozen wasteland ahead.

Hour 46 to 48: The Gates of the Coward

I stepped out of the SUV. The Himalayan wind hit me like a physical blow, biting through my thin clothing. I didn't care. The cold only sharpened my focus.

The timer ticked to Hour 49. The final siege had begun.
