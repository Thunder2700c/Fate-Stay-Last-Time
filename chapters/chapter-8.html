Hour 73: The King's First Meal

The Himalayan wind howled against the heavy oak doors of the monastery, but inside, the war of gods and kings was momentarily suspended by the screech of a cheap portable gas stove.

Jaskaran, still wearing the heavy, intimidating cassock of a Holy Church Executor, was squatting on the stone floor, pouring boiling water into three styrofoam cups of instant noodles.

He handed the first one to Saber.

She took it hesitantly with both hands. For the first time since I summoned her, the King of Knights wasn't radiating a faint, ethereal light. She was sitting on a wooden pew, wrapped in a thick wool blanket, physically shivering. The cold of the mountain was biting into her newly formed, mortal flesh.

She stared down at the steaming cup. Slowly, she took a bite.

Her emerald eyes widened slightly. A faint flush of color returned to her pale, freezing cheeks. It wasn't a grand feast of Camelot. It was cheap, sodium-filled college food. But to a spirit who had lived for centuries as nothing but a weapon, the simple, agonizingly beautiful warmth of the broth spreading through her chest was overwhelming.

She didn't say a word. She just pulled the blanket tighter around her shoulders, closed her eyes, and kept eating, holding onto the cup like it was a lifeline.

Jaskaran handed me a cup and sat down on the stone steps of the altar, blowing on his broth.

Jaskaran

"You know... we had that PTU fourth-semester exam tomorrow. Financial Management. I hadn't even opened the syllabus."

I let out a dry, exhausted laugh, the sound echoing in the empty church.

Gurtej

"Yeah. I guess we're dropping out of GNIMT."

Jaskaran

"Honestly? Thank God. I hated that class anyway."

Hour 74: The Psychopath Pact

We ate in silence for a few minutes, the sheer absurdity of the situation settling over us. The entire planet was hunting us. The United Nations was deploying armies. The Clock Tower was sending its elite executioners.

Jaskaran

"Gurtej. When we were walking up here, you said you wanted to wipe the board clean. Were you just talking, or did you mean it?"

His voice had dropped the casual college tone, returning to the cold cadence of an Overseer.

I looked at him. I remembered the mob of civilians turning on Saber in the marketplace. I remembered the military gunships slaughtering their own people just to frame us for the collateral damage. The mediator was dead. This timeline was a rotting corpse.

Gurtej

"The Magi, the politicians, the Church... they're all corrupt. If we win this Grail, I'm not wishing for money or a reset. I want to erase it all."

I waited for the righteous Executor to draw his blades. I waited for my best friend to tell me I had lost my mind.

Instead, Jaskaran just took another slurp of his noodles. He swallowed, wiped his mouth, and looked at me with dead, empty eyes.

Jaskaran

"Valid."

I blinked. "Valid? That's it?"

Jaskaran

"Bro, I've worked for the Burial Agency for three years. Do you know the kind of sick, twisted garbage the Magus families do behind closed doors? Do you know how much blood the Church spills just to keep the 'masquerade' intact? The world is a meat grinder. If you want to break the wheel, I'm with you."

Two college dropouts, sitting in a freezing church, casually agreeing to global annihilation.

Hour 75: The Forge Awakens

Gurtej

"Then we need to split our forces. I have two contracts. It's too much of a target. You need to take Archer."

Archer, who had been sitting quietly against a stone pillar, opened one gray eye.

Archer

"A Master transfer. Tactical. I have no objections, provided this fake priest can supply the mana."

Jaskaran

"I'm anchoring you to the mountain's leyline, you arrogant bastard. You'll have infinite mana. You stay here, defend the church, and cover us with long-range Broken Phantasms. Gurtej and Saber will be the vanguard."

Jaskaran walked over to me, holding out his hand to begin the formal severance ritual. "Alright, Gurtej. Release the contract."

I closed my eyes and focused on the magical tether linking my soul to the red-coated archer. Sever.

The moment the spiritual connection snapped, a violent, agonizing heat exploded up my right arm. I screamed, falling to my knees on the stone floor. It wasn't the pain of losing a Servant. It was the feeling of something waking up inside my own blood.

The air in the church suddenly tasted heavy—like ozone and rusted iron. The stone floor beneath my knees began to thrum with a deep, mechanical heartbeat.

Crack.

The sheer density of the mana leaking from my body caused three of the glass prayer candles on the altar to physically shatter.

Saber

"Gurtej!"

Saber dropped her blanket, rushing to my side, her instincts as a knight overriding her mortal fragility.

Jaskaran

"Stand back!"

I ripped up the sleeve of my jacket. My skin was glowing. Intricate, neon-green lines of magical circuitry were burning themselves permanently into my flesh, pulsing with the rhythmic sound of grinding metal gears.

Archer

"The Reality Marble... Because the Grail forced my forge open using his life force, my Magecraft didn't just drain him. It burned an imprint into his soul."

Archer pushed himself off the pillar, genuine astonishment on his face.

I stared at my glowing hands. The image of the ash-choked wasteland, the infinite swords, the turning gears in the sky—it was locked inside my head. I could feel the structure, the composition, the history of the weapons.

I raised my glowing right hand.

"Trace — On."

Hour 76: The Mastermind Play

Jaskaran stared at the sword in my hand, a manic grin spreading across his face.

Jaskaran

"Okay. Okay, this changes things. With Saber unsealed as a mortal and you wielding Projection magic, you guys can actually fight. But we need to thin the herd before you leave."

He walked back to the altar, sorting through the Clock Tower dossiers.

Jaskaran

"As the Overseer, the other Masters are required to report to me. I'm going to play the terrified, neutral priest. When the Masters of Berserker and the High Assassin contact the Church, I'll 'accidentally' leak Gilgamesh's coordinates. I'll convince them the Golden King is the ultimate threat. Let the monsters tear each other apart while I sit here drinking coffee."

Archer

"Devious. You truly are fit for the Holy Church."

Hour 77: The Planet's Immune System

I let the projected sword dissolve into blue ether.

Gurtej

"So Gilgamesh is dealt with for now. What's the final boss?"

Jaskaran stopped smiling. The manic energy left his eyes, replaced by a cold dread.

Jaskaran

"The Earth itself."

Saber frowned, stepping forward. "The Earth?"

Jaskaran

"Listen to me carefully. Imagine the planet Earth is a human body. Right now, humanity is like a virus living on that body. The UN armies, the Clock Tower Magi... they are just a fever. They are human problems. But if we actually get close to winning the Grail... if we actually try to erase humanity..."

Jaskaran tapped the center of the map.
